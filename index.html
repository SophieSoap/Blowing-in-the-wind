Wind energy is Germany's primary renewable energy source, essential for the nation's shift away from fossil fuels. With one of the world's largest capacities for onshore wind generation, Germany has made significant strides in efficiency and cost reduction.

Despite the growth of wind energy, there has also been increasing social pushback threatening the potential to have wind continue to grow into a crucial component of Germany's energy transition. As Germany grapples with these challenges, the question arises:

What are the key arguments hindering the progress of wind energy?

Argument 1

Negative effects on wildlife and nature

The biggest pushback against wind energy is due to environmental concerns. The German economy ministry (BMWi) has pointed to the Federal Ministry of the Environment (BMU) as being responsible for blocking the expansion of wind energy. Legal challenges and regulatory changes have further complicated the landscape, posing a formidable challenge to meeting emissions reduction goals. Here are some of the nature conservation-based arguments against the development of wind energy in Germany, and the voices of people and organizations behind such arguments.

1. Pressure changes

The construction of wind farms can lead to habitat fragmentation, disrupting ecosystems and wildlife areas. This can isolate animal populations, reducing genetic diversity and hindering natural migration, notably affecting bats and birds.

“The impact of a wind turbine on its environment is significantly greater than the sealed area alone. Many animals are killed by wind turbines, especially bats, birds and insects.” NABU, Landesverband Berlin [15]

“If there are a lot of wind turbines in the way, the detours can lead to the birds becoming exhausted. The effects of this increased effort can be that some animals do not reach their breeding area in the south or miss the right time to breed.” NABU, Landesverband Berlin [15]

Turbines pose a threat to certain bat species, creating a conflict between the development of renewable energy sources and the conservation of endangered species, often called a “green-green dilemma.” [19]

Even without direct contact, bats can be killed by barotrauma on wind turbines. This is an injury caused by pressure changes that is fatal for bats. [15]

2. Hindering nature conservation policy

The construction of wind farms can sometimes conflict with the interest of nature conservation plans.

Environmental impact beyond perimeter of wind farms: 45 lawsuits were initiated by NABU between 2010 and 2019 due to incorrect planning for wind energy. Not because the expansion of wind energy should be stopped, but because projects and plans repeatedly blatantly violate nature conservation law. [16]

"In times of probably the greatest loss of biodiversity since the extinction of the dinosaurs, this is a terrible, fatal development," Annette Müller-Zietzke, a local fighting the development of wind turbines in sections of the Reinhardswald, a forest in Hessen [16]

Argument 2

Community Concerns: Health, Wellbeing & Aesthetics

The second most significant barrier to expanding wind energy in Germany is local opposition from residents living near wind farms. Individual initiatives have proven to be effective in voicing concerns, and here are some of their arguments:

1. Impact on physical and mental health

Wind power critics argue turbines also threaten human physical and mental health, especially due to their infrasonic emissions.

“Wind Turbine Syndrome”, The term was coined nine years ago by the American pediatrician Nina Pierpont. She had collected case stories from patients who lived near wind turbines and published them in a book. The complaints are the same that AEFIS hears from its patients today. The list has now only become longer: sleep disorders, dizziness, nausea, high blood pressure, concentration disorders, tinnitus, fatigue, depression, heart rhythm disorders, anxiety disorders... [13]

“Almost everyone complains of insomnia. There are also complaints of severe earaches, headaches, palpitations, dizziness, dizziness during the day and balance problems. “You have a feeling of trepidation in your chest and you can't fall asleep. You think you're about to lose your breath. Then you look in the mirror in the morning and see a burst vein in your eye, so I've had high blood pressure for several years now. Now I've also had tinnitus for a year.” -Regina Dietz, resident of the Etteln district [13]

“The most comprehensive study to date on the effects of infrasound on humans was published by the Federal Environment Agency in 2014. It states: A detailed analysis of the available literature shows that sound concentrated in the low-frequency range can significantly impair mental well-being even at low levels. The effect intensifies with an increasing shift to low frequencies up to the infrasound range.” [13]

Additional NoteLow-frequency sound refers to the so-called hearing sound and infrasound. They are measured from 100 Hz down to 10 Hz. From a frequency of approx. 20 - 16 Hz, the hearing sound passes into the infrasound that is no longer audible to humans. The lower its frequency, the further it is transmitted.

2. Turbine Microplastics causing Future Health Concerns

The disposal of decommissioned turbine components presents environmental and health challenges, as current procedures do not meet standards for hazardous waste management. Rotors are susceptible to erosion, releasing toxic microplastics into soils, and the spread of bisphenol A (BPA) from epoxy resin presents additional risks.

The use of carbon fiber-reinforced plastics (CFP) in wind turbine rotors, known as "naughty fibers," poses risks akin to asbestos. In the event of a fire, these materials must be carefully managed due to the toxic microparticles they release, which can cause lung cancer upon inhalation. With the government's plans for widespread wind turbine installations near settlements, concerns arise about increased lightning strikes and subsequent fires, compounded by the absence of civil protection plans. [21]

3. Aesthetics

Wind farms contain a multitude of large wind turbines that have a big impact on the aesthetics of the natural landscape where they are developed.

"For me, the main reason is still justice, distributive justice, that this can't be right, that there are in fact no more free lines of sight, that drives people crazy, that drives them mad. They grew up here with a very wide view, these are plateaus...now wherever you look: The area is plastered with wind turbines. There are around 500 in the district. Gray colossal towers into the sky everywhere, surrounding the villages. If the investors had their way - as the mayor is sure they will - the number would double again." -Reiner Allerdissen, the mayor of Borchen, a municipality in the Paderborn district with a population of 13,000. [13]

“Wind turbines are undoubtedly changing landscapes. The towers are getting higher and higher, with the tips of the rotors reaching up to 250 meters in height. In good weather, the light gray turbines are visible from afar.” [11]

"At first, I actually found wind turbines quite exciting as a technological innovation and aesthetically pleasing as a building. But then when I traveled to Denmark, I saw them: The more of these things there were in a pile, the more the hairs on the back of my neck stood on end because I saw them: It's like a forest of poles." [12]

Argument 3

It Doesn’t Make Sense Financially

Another big pushback against the growth of wind energy in Germany is the costs to make it happen, a big hurdle for the transition to renewable energy generation in general. The implementation of wind energy requires funding for not only the wind power generators themselves (wind farms), but also requires large investments into an infrastructure to help store and distribute this energy. Here are some of the arguments that say it’s not worth the investment:

1. The costs to build an energy distribution infrastructure is being passed down to end consumers.

The fees to build the new infrastructures for distributing renewable energy are often passed on to residents in the areas where the infrastructure for wind energy is concentrated.

"It is unacceptable that the considerable costs of Germany's energy transition are only borne by consumers in the north. " -Tom Janneck, Mitarbeiter der Verbraucherzentrale Schleswig-Holstein. [9]

Grid fees currently make up about a quarter of the final electricity price paid by consumers, which typically varies between €0.30 and €0.40 per kilowatt hour. But the fees differ considerably as consumers in regions with high construction costs have to pay more to compensate operators of regional distribution grids for building new power lines. [4]

Additional Note: The construction of wind turbines alone is not enough. Large wind farms in particular are located in the north of Germany, but the largest consumers are in the center and south of the country. In order to be able to transport the large amounts of energy, the grid needs new lines. According to the calculations in the NEP, the costs for grid expansion amount to around 55 billion euros. This sum includes measures for the initial grid, the expansion grid and power factor correction systems. [6]

2. In order to efficiently use wind energy, we need to invest even more in inefficient energy storage infrastructure.

The naturally fluctuating output of wind turbines makes it challenging to balance supply and demand of wind energy. Pumped hydro and battery storage are intended to counteract this issue. However, their potential is often overestimated and currently can only store a fraction of the excess electricity. [7]

"If the German pump and battery storage systems had to take over the power supply, the storage systems would be empty after less than half an hour; the battery storage systems installed today would be empty after less than a minute. [...] A hypothetical equalization of wind generation to its average value would require a storage capacity that is already far above what can foreseeably be realized as storage capacity." - Bundesnetzagentur ( "Federal Network Agency")
